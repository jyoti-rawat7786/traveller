Agra, Mathura & Vrindavan Tour with Delhi — Simple 3-Day Guide

A clear, easy-to-follow plan for first-time visitors. This guide uses simple English and gives a friendly schedule, travel tips, places to see and practical advice for a relaxed 3-day trip.

Why this trip works well

This short tour covers Delhi, Agra, Mathura and Vrindavan. It mixes history, culture, temples and calm walks. You will see the world-famous Taj Mahal, the red stone beauty of Agra Fort, the holy places of Mathura and the devotional mood of Vrindavan. The distances are short and the roads are good, so you can move comfortably between places in three days.

Best Time to Visit

To enjoy the trip comfortably, choose these months:

  • October to March: Cool and pleasant days — best for sightseeing.
  • April to June: Hot and sunny — early morning visits are better.
  • July to September: Monsoon — fewer crowds but expect rain and humid weather.

Morning and late afternoon are the best times for outdoor sightseeing because days can get warm.

How to Reach & Move Around

All four places are well connected by road and rail. Here are easy options:

Arriving in Delhi

Delhi has the main international and domestic airports (Indira Gandhi International). From Delhi you can start the tour by car or train. Many travellers prefer to begin in Delhi and then head to Agra by road or by train.

Delhi to Agra

  • By road: 200–230 km, about 3.5 to 5 hours depending on the route and traffic.
  • By train: Fast trains like Gatimaan Express and Shatabdi connect Delhi and Agra (around 1.5 to 3 hours).

Agra to Mathura & Vrindavan

Mathura is about 55–60 km from Agra. Vrindavan is just 15–20 km from Mathura. You can hire a private car or take local trains and buses. A private car gives you more time and ease for temple visits.

Tip: For a smooth trip, hire a trusted driver or use a reputable tour operator who knows local timings and parking spots near monuments.

Suggested 3-Day Itinerary (Simple)

Day 1 — Delhi: Quick City Highlights

Start your day in Delhi. If you arrive early, visit a few top spots like Qutub Minar, India Gate, Humayun’s Tomb or the busy market of Chandni Chowk. Keep this day light if you plan to leave for Agra in the evening or next morning. Rest well — the real sightseeing begins at Agra.

Day 2 — Agra: Taj Mahal, Agra Fort, Mehtab Bagh

  • Early morning: Visit the Taj Mahal at sunrise for a magical and less crowded view. Spend time walking around the gardens and the main tomb area.
  • Late morning: Have breakfast and then visit Agra Fort — a large red-sandstone fort with beautiful palaces and views of the Yamuna river.
  • Afternoon: Enjoy local food at a nearby restaurant; rest in your hotel if needed. Visit local marble shops or markets for souvenirs.
  • Evening: Head to Mehtab Bagh (across the river) for a gentle view of the Taj at sunset.

Day 3 — Mathura & Vrindavan: Temples and Devotion

  • Morning in Mathura: Mathura is the birthplace of Lord Krishna. Visit Shri Krishna Janmabhoomi temple and the local ghats by the Yamuna river. The morning rituals are full of color and devotion.
  • Late morning to afternoon: Move to Vrindavan (about 30 minutes). Vrindavan is full of temples and peaceful lanes. Visit Banke Bihari Temple, ISKCON Temple, Radha Raman Temple and Prem Mandir (if you prefer a modern marble temple with light shows in the evening).
  • Evening: Enjoy the aarti (prayer) at the ghats or at a major temple — these ceremonies are an important part of the local experience.

Places Not to Miss

  • Taj Mahal (Agra): The symbol of love and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Best at sunrise or early morning.
  • Agra Fort: A strong and beautiful Mughal fort with palaces and gardens inside.
  • Shri Krishna Janmabhoomi (Mathura): The sacred site believed to be the birthplace of Lord Krishna.
  • Banke Bihari Temple (Vrindavan): A lively and devotional temple with a unique energetic feel.
  • Prem Mandir (Vrindavan): A modern marble temple known for its evening light and sound show.

Food & Where to Eat

The region serves a mix of North Indian food: chole bhature, dal, rice, rotis, kebabs and sweet treats. Street food in Mathura and Vrindavan is famous for sweets like peda. In Agra, try local Mughlai dishes and the popular petha sweet. Always choose clean and busy places for a safer food experience.

Practical Tips — Easy and Useful

  • Carry drinking water and a hat during hot months.
  • Wear comfortable shoes — you will walk a lot on uneven paths.
  • Respect religious customs: dress modestly and remove shoes when required inside temples.
  • Book Taj Mahal tickets online in advance to skip long queues.
  • Keep small cash notes for local transport, donations and small shop purchases.
  • If you are driving, use a reliable GPS and allow extra time for traffic.

Safety & Health

Overall the area is safe for tourists. Follow these common-sense tips:

  • Do not leave belongings unattended at crowded places.
  • Keep emergency numbers and hotel contact saved.
  • If you have health issues, carry medicine and consult local hospitals if needed — carry a basic first-aid kit.

Local Culture & Simple Etiquette

The cities in this trip have rich history and religious life. Here are simple points to follow:

  • Ask permission before photographing people, especially during prayers.
  • Use both hands when giving or receiving items at a temple or from an elder as a sign of respect.
  • Speak softly in holy places and avoid loud music or shouting.

Where to Stay

Choose a hotel near the city center or close to the Taj for Agra. In Mathura and Vrindavan, many pilgrims stay in simple guest houses or dharamshalas. If you prefer comfort, pick mid-range hotels in Nainital or Agra with good reviews and easy access to transport.
