Quick overview

Chardham is a famous Hindu pilgrimage circuit in Uttarakhand made of four sacred shrines: Yamunotri, Gangotri, Kedarnath and Badrinath. Most pilgrims start from Delhi because it connects well by road, rail and air. A Delhi-to-Chardham yatra needs planning because of long drives, mountain roads and seasonal weather. This guide gives you the best route choices, approximate distances, and clear itinerary options for different travel speeds.

Best starting point and route choices

From Delhi you can start the yatra by road (car/coach), by train to Haridwar or Rishikesh, or fly to Dehradun and continue by road. There are two main route styles commonly used by tour operators and pilgrims:

1. The classic clockwise circuit (most common)

Delhi → Haridwar/Rishikesh → Yamunotri → Gangotri → Kedarnath → Badrinath → Return via Rishikesh/Haridwar/Delhi. This route flows geographically and minimizes back-and-forth driving.

2. Counter-clockwise or mixed circuits

Operators sometimes reverse the order to match road openings, helicopter slots, or to manage weather. Helicopter transfers to Kedarnath also allow mixed routing where you save time on the Kedarnath leg.

Suggested itineraries from Delhi (three options)

Choose an itinerary based on how quickly you want to travel and whether you prefer helicopter help for Kedarnath. Below are three popular plans: Fast (8–9 days), Comfortable (10–12 days), and Slow/Leisurely (13+ days).

Option A — Fast Pace (8–9 days)

  1. Day 1: Delhi → Haridwar (overnight in Haridwar/Rishikesh)
  2. Day 2: Haridwar → Barkot (base for Yamunotri)
  3. Day 3: Yamunotri darshan → Barkot → Uttarkashi
  4. Day 4: Uttarkashi → Gangotri darshan → Guptkashi (towards Kedarnath)
  5. Day 5: Sonprayag → Trek/Heli to Kedarnath, darshan, overnight
  6. Day 6: Descend to Sonprayag → Drive to Joshimath → Badrinath
  7. Day 7: Badrinath darshan → Return towards Rishikesh/Haridwar
  8. Day 8: Travel back to Delhi

Fast packages have long drive days; ideal if you have limited time and are comfortable with quick travel.

Option B — Comfortable Pace (10–12 days)

  1. Day 1: Delhi → Haridwar/Rishikesh
  2. Day 2: Haridwar → Barkot (rest)
  3. Day 3: Yamunotri darshan → Barkot
  4. Day 4: Barkot → Uttarkashi (rest)
  5. Day 5: Uttarkashi → Gangotri darshan → Guptkashi
  6. Day 6: Guptkashi → Sonprayag → Trek to Kedarnath (or heli)
  7. Day 7: Kedarnath darshan → Sonprayag → Drive to Joshimath
  8. Day 8: Joshimath → Badrinath darshan
  9. Day 9: Badrinath → Return to Rishikesh
  10. Day 10: Rishikesh → Haridwar → Delhi

This pace includes rest days and shorter drives each day — better for families and older travellers.

Option C — Leisurely/Relaxed (13+ days)

Extend the Comfortable plan by adding buffer days for weather, longer stays at Uttarkashi, local sightseeing, or changing Kedarnath trek to a two-day walk with an overnight stop. This option is best if you prefer no rush, time to acclimatize, and to enjoy local culture.

Helicopter tips for Kedarnath (if you choose to use heli)

  • Book early through official portals (IRCTC Heli or authorised operators) — seats sell out fast in May–June.
  • Heli reduces the 16 km trek to just short walks from the landing point, saving time and physical effort.
  • Keep buffer days — flights are weather dependent and can be cancelled on short notice.
  • Expect luggage limits and strict check-in times at helipads. Carry ID and booking confirmations.

Practical travel tips & safety

Health & altitude

  • Acclimatize slowly: include rest days before high-altitude segments like Kedarnath and Badrinath.
  • Stay hydrated and eat light during travel days.
  • Carry basic medicines: paracetamol, motion sickness tablets, ORS, blister plasters, and altitude-relief meds if prescribed.

Road safety

  • Mountain roads are narrow and winding. Use experienced drivers and avoid night driving.
  • Check vehicle condition and confirm driver’s experience on mountain routes with your operator.

Local rules & etiquette

  • Dress modestly for temple visits, follow queue rules and avoid loud behavior near shrines.
  • Carry small cash — ATMs can be scarce on the route and may not always work.

Packing list — essential items

  • Warm layers: fleece, light down jacket, thermal wear
  • Comfortable walking shoes, socks and a pair of sandals
  • Rainproof jacket, small umbrella (for monsoon months)
  • Power bank, torch, chargers and spare batteries
  • Personal medicines, first-aid kit and hand sanitizer
  • ID proofs, photocopies, and printed booking confirmations
  • Small offerings for temples and enough cash for local needs

Budget estimate (simple)

Costs depend on vehicle type, hotels, helicopter choices and season. Here is a rough per-person guide for a shared package:

  • Shared Chardham package (10–12 days, mid-range hotels): ₹20,000 – ₹35,000
  • Private vehicle or higher comfort: ₹45,000 – ₹80,000+
  • Helicopter seat to Kedarnath: ₹6,000 – ₹12,000 extra (per seat, varies)
  • Food & small expenses: ₹3,000 – ₹6,000 depending on choices

These are sample figures — ask your operator for a detailed price breakdown and confirm what is included: hotel, meals, permits, driver allowances and helicopter if chosen.
