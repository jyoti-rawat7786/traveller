What is Chardham?

Chardham refers to four sacred Hindu pilgrimage sites in Uttarakhand: Yamunotri, Gangotri, Kedarnath and Badrinath. Pilgrims visit these shrines seeking spiritual blessings. The route is scenic with high mountain views, rivers and simple hill towns. A Chardham tour commonly refers to a single trip covering all four shrines, usually arranged by travel operators as a package that includes transport, hotels and local assistance.

Why choose a package?

Chardham routes involve long drives, mountain roads and tight schedules. Booking a package can make the trip easier because a tour operator will manage:

  • Pickup and drop from a chosen city (often Delhi or Dehradun)
  • Hotel bookings along the route
  • Local transport including experienced drivers familiar with mountain roads
  • Guides or help for temple formalities and local needs
  • Emergency support and backup plans in case of weather or road issues

Packages reduce planning stress and are a practical choice for many first-time pilgrims.

Best Time to Visit

The Chardham route is seasonal because high passes close in winter due to snow. Common travel windows are:

  • Opening season (May to June): Many pilgrims start in May when roads open and weather warms up.
  • Peak season (June to September): Summer months are popular but monsoon (July–August) can bring road slips; operators often plan flexible routes during rains.
  • October to early November: Post-monsoon months give clear skies and cooler days — another good window before winter closures.

Always check the specific year’s opening dates as local authorities announce road openings and closures.

How people usually travel (routes)

There are two main circuit styles for Chardham tours:

1. The usual clockwise circuit

  • Start at  Haridwar / Rishikesh
  • Yamunotri → Uttarkashi → Gangotri → Guptkashi → Kedarnath → Badrinath → Joshimath → Return

2. Alternative counter-clockwise or mixed routes

Some packages change the order for weather, road conditions or to shorten travel days. Operators may also combine helicopter transfers (popular for Kedarnath if roads are slow) to save time.

Sample Itineraries (Simple Options)

8–9 Day Express Package (Popular)

This is for travellers who want to cover Chardham quickly with longer travel days. Example flow:

  • Day 1: Drive from Haridwar/Rishikesh to Barkot
  • Day 2: Yamunotri darshan and return to Uttarkashi
  • Day 3: Uttarkashi to Gangotri — darshan — then move towards Guptkashi
  • Day 4: Drive to Sonprayag and then trek/drive to Kedarnath or take helicopter (depending on package)
  • Day 5: Kedarnath darshan — back to Sonprayag and head to Badrinath route
  • Day 6: Badrinath darshan and local visit
  • Day 7: Return journey towards Haridwar with stops
  • Day 8: Arrival back at Haridwar/Delhi

11–12 Day Comfort Package

This allows shorter driving days and time to rest:

  • Includes longer stays in Uttarkashi, Guptkashi and Joshimath
  • Allows time for local sightseeing and recovery from altitude
  • Often includes optional activities such as short treks or local markets

Choose an itinerary based on how much driving you can comfortably do and whether you want helicopter options for Kedarnath.

Helicopter vs Road — What to consider

Many travellers choose helicopter transfer to Kedarnath for time or health reasons. Points to consider:

  • Helicopter: Faster, less physically demanding, higher cost, weather-dependent and seats limited.
  • By road/trek: Cheaper, allows local views, but involves long drives and for Kedarnath a final trek or pony ride if you don’t use helicopter.

Helicopter choices need early booking in peak season. Road trips provide a deeper local experience but require better stamina.

What to Pack — Practical List

  • Warm layers (fleece or light down) — nights and mornings can be cold
  • Comfortable walking shoes and a pair of sandals
  • Rainproof jacket and a small umbrella during monsoon months
  • Sun protection: hat, sunglasses and sunscreen
  • Basic medicines, water purifier tablets and motion-sickness pills if you are prone to it
  • Small torch, power bank and necessary chargers
  • Simple offerings for the temples and small cash notes for local purchases

Health, Altitude and Safety

Chardham visits include high-altitude areas (Kedarnath and Badrinath are higher than Yamunotri and Gangotri). Simple safety tips:

  • Stay hydrated and avoid alcohol during travel days — dehydration increases altitude issues
  • Move slowly and avoid sudden exertion on the first day in higher altitudes
  • If you feel severe shortness of breath, dizziness or persistent headache, inform your guide immediately
  • Carry basic first-aid and the phone numbers of local medical centres. Travel operators usually help with emergency arrangements.

Accommodation & Food

Packages usually include hotel stays in small towns along the route. Options range from budget guesthouses to mid-range hotels. Food is generally simple and local; most hotels serve Indian vegetarian and non-vegetarian meals. If you have special dietary needs, tell your operator in advance.

Costs — What to Expect

Package costs vary widely based on season, vehicle type, inclusion of helicopter transfers, and hotel choices. Typical factors that increase price:

  • Peak season travel and week-ends
  • Helicopter transfer to Kedarnath
  • Private vehicle instead of shared coach
  • Higher category hotels

Ask for a clear price breakdown before booking so you know what is included: accommodation, meals, entry fees (if any), helicopter (if chosen), and any extra charges for road delays.

Booking Tips & Questions to Ask

  • Check the operator’s local experience and read recent reviews.
  • Ask about cancelation policies and weather contingency plans.
  • Confirm vehicle type, driver experience and daily driving hours.
  • Ask if permits or registration are required for any shrine — operators usually handle this but confirm.
  • Check whether porter or pony services are available for the Kedarnath trek if needed.
