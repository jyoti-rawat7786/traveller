Rishikesh Rafting Tour Package — Easy Guide

A friendly and simple guide on river rafting in Rishikesh. Learn about packages, safety, packing list, sample itineraries and what to expect — all written in clear, easy English for first-time travellers.

Why Raft in Rishikesh?

Rishikesh is known as India's adventure capital for its beautiful river and mountain setting. The Ganges flows fast here and offers good rapids for rafting. The sport is fun, safe when done with a good operator, and fits all levels — from gentle floats to challenging rapids. Many travelers combine rafting with yoga, camping, or short treks to make a full adventure trip.

Types of Rafting Packages

Most operators offer different packages to fit your time and comfort. Common types:

  • Short/Beginner stretches: 6–8 km, gentle rapids, ideal for families and first-timers.
  • Standard/Popular stretches: 12–16 km, moderate rapids and a full experience of the river.
  • Long/Advanced stretches: 25 km or more, for experienced rafters and adventure seekers.
  • Combo packages: Rafting with camping, bonfire, local food or yoga sessions included.

Choose a package based on your fitness level and how much time you have.

What a Typical Package Includes

  • Pickup from Rishikesh town or a nearby meeting point
  • Transport to the rafting start point
  • All rafting equipment: life jackets, helmets, paddles and rubber boats
  • Guided rafting with trained instructors
  • Basic insurance as provided by the operator
  • Return transfer to Rishikesh after rafting

Extra services like photos, videos, meals, camping tents and overnight stays may be added for a fee.

How to Book — Step by Step

  1. Pick a trusted operator with good reviews and visible safety records.
  2. Decide the stretch and time you prefer — morning trips are cooler and less crowded.
  3. Check what is included and confirm pickup location and time.
  4. Book in advance especially during weekends, holidays, and peak months (Oct–Mar).
  5. Bring photo ID on the day and arrive at the meeting point on time.

Safety — Important Points

Rafting is safe when followed with rules. Key safety points:

  • Always wear a life jacket and helmet — these are provided by the operator.
  • Listen to the guide and follow paddle commands during the ride.
  • Do not stand in the boat; stay seated and keep your feet inside.
  • Operators run a short safety talk and demonstrate rescue methods — pay attention.
  • If you fall in, float on your back with feet downstream and wait for rescue instructions.

Note: People with serious medical conditions, recent surgery, or pregnancy should avoid rafting. Check with your doctor and tell the operator about any health issue.

Sample One-Day Itinerary (Standard 12–16 km)

This plan fits most travellers who start from Rishikesh town.

  • 07:30 AM: Pickup from hotel or meeting point in Rishikesh.
  • 08:00 AM: Reach base camp — sign papers and listen to safety briefing.
  • 09:00 AM: Drive to the rafting start point and prepare gear.
  • 09:30 AM – 12:00 PM: Rafting on the chosen stretch with a guide and short breaks.
  • 12:00 – 01:00 PM: Finish at the take-out point, change clothes, have lunch (if included) and return to Rishikesh.
  • 02:00 PM: Back in Rishikesh — rest or join other activities like cafe visits or yoga.

Packing List — What to Carry

  • Swimwear or quick-dry clothes (wear under your clothes)
  • Sports shoes or water shoes with a firm grip (no flip-flops during rafting)
  • Change of dry clothes and a towel
  • Sunscreen, sunglasses (strap to hold them) and a hat for waiting time
  • Photo ID (original), some cash and a small waterproof bag for phone/cash

Costs & Booking Tips

Rafting prices vary by stretch, season and operator. Some tips to save time and money:

  • Book online for clear pricing and confirmed slots.
  • Compare operators for safety standards and customer reviews rather than the cheapest price.
  • Group bookings often get small discounts.
  • Ask about inclusive items (meals, photos) to avoid surprises on the day.

Other Activities to Combine

  • Zip-lining and giant swing in nearby adventure parks
  • Camping by the river with bonfire and local food
  • Yoga sessions and meditation in Rishikesh’s ashrams
  • Short treks to nearby viewpoints and waterfalls

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Is rafting safe for beginners? Yes — on beginner and standard stretches with a trained guide, rafting is safe for most healthy people.
  • What happens if it rains? Operators may postpone trips if the river is too dangerous. Rain may increase river flow — safety decisions are made by the guide.
  • Can I take my phone on the boat? It is not recommended unless it is in a secured waterproof case; many operators offer photos afterwards.
  • Do I need to know swimming? No — life jackets are mandatory and they help even non-swimmers, but knowing basic swimming helps confidence.
